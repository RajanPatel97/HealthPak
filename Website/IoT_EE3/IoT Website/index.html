HealthPak

HealthPak is an all-in-one solution to enable suppliers and consumers to accurately monitor the transport environment of sensitive goods. Our specific focus is the safe transport of medicines, organs and other health products which require strict environments for preservation.

Why HealthPak?

We've got what you need!

HealthPak is versatile, bespoke enclosure that measures vibration, humidity, ambient temperature and surface temperature. HealthPak communicates data online, allowing the user to access real-time information on the contents of the enclosure at any time and warning them when and what has gone wrong or if the environment is as nominal.

What's inside of HealthPak?

General information

HealthPak uses 3 different sensors to continuously measure the temperature, humidity and vibrations inside of the smart cooling box. The sensors communicate with a WiFi chip in order to transmit the data they have collected to a Broker using Message Queuing Telemetry Transport (MQTT) communication. Any device that requires data from the sensors can subscribe to that Broker through a certain channel making it available to any online interface. As well as our website and potential App, we also have a User Interface consisting of a Light Emitting Diode and a Buzzer subscribing to the Broker through its own WiFi chip. This UI provides an easier and quicker way to monitor HealthPak's parameters as it changes color and makes sound when anything goes wrong.

Looking to the Future...

We currently have a seperate recieving device which has 2 RGB LED's that represent whether temperature and humidity inside the HealthPak are too high or nominal respectively. A Piezo Buzzer sounds an alarm if there is too much vibration. In the future, we aim to develop our product further by harnessing our current feedback mechanism to add heating/cooling elements, as well as a dehumidifer. We want these additons to provide a complete closed loop control system that automatically corrects any environmental differences inside the HealthPak.

Technical specifications

ESP8266

The ESP8266 is our WiFi chip. The board on which it sits also has some flash memory, an antenna and all the necessary circuitry for powering and USB interfacing. It's purpose in our design is to send out measured data to the Broker to make it available online.

TMP007

The TMP007 is a Thermophile sensor that measures radiated energy from an object. We use it to see if the temperature of the stored goods is adequate, regardless of the ambient temperature of the box.

Si7021

The Si7021 is a temperature and humidity sensor. It measures whether the environment within the cooling box is appropriate.

LIS3DH

The LIS3DH is an accelerometer. Since some goods are fragile or sensitive to sudden movements, our design contains a sensor capable of measuring acceleration.

Our Motivation...

Why is HealthPak needed?

With vast technological advances in goods shipment and delivery, we are now able to track the location of our purchases at any given time. In the case of sensitive goods that are stored in refrigerators such as medicine, organs or even some foods, however, the manufacturer, retailer or consumer might be interested in the conditions in which these goods were stored during transportation. Vaccines or organs, for instance, require an ambient temperature of 2 to 8ºC. If the cooling box's temperature is outside these bounds, the vaccine loses effectiveness. This can cause the consumer to lose trust in the product, cost a lot of money for the manufacturer and may be detrimental for a consumer whose life is heavily reliant on medicines or a transplant. HealthPak measures this data and makes it available for anyone involved with the shipped goods enhancing customer relationships, allowing retailers to discard any wrongly stored goods and providing intel to manufacturers on any reoccurring storage problems.

There are currently over 4 million people in the UK living with Diabetes, along with 60,000 IVF treatments taking place every year. In the US, 21 million blood components are transfused each year. Many of the treatments for illnesses, genetic defeciencies and diseases require specific and strict storage and transportation. Current technology is very basic in this field and more advanced designs are extremely expensive and cannot be bought on mass. This is where HealthPak fits in, to fill the gap in this market.
